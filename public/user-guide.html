Overview

The Run Sheet Generator reads scanned oil and gas deed and lease PDFs, automatically extracts legal instruments using AI, and produces a formatted Chain of Title Excel spreadsheet in a fraction of the time it would take manually.

Upload your PDFs, review and correct the extracted data, then export a ready-to-use Excel file sorted by your preferred date field and order.

Bulk uploads supported. Files upload directly from your browser to cloud storage — there is no file size limit. You can process large batches of 25 or more documents at once.
Always review before exporting. The AI is a first-pass tool. Legal descriptions, handwritten documents, and complex instruments will require manual correction.

Step 2 — Upload Your PDFs

Click the Upload PDFs file picker and select one or more PDF files. Hold Ctrl (Windows) or Cmd (Mac) to select multiple files at once.

No file size limit. Files upload directly from your browser to secure cloud storage, bypassing all server size restrictions. Large PDFs and large batches are fully supported.
PDFs only. The tool only accepts .pdf files. Images and Word documents are not supported.
Name files descriptively. The source filename appears in the review table, so names like DB_261-445.pdf make it easier to trace back to the original document.

Confidence Color Coding

Each row is color-coded based on how confident the AI is in its extraction. Always review low-confidence rows before exporting.

High

All fields extracted cleanly from a clear, typed document. Minimal review needed.

Medium

Most fields found but something was unclear. Review recommended.

Low

Significant uncertainty — handwriting, heavy degradation, or missing fields. Always review manually.

Notes for Reviewer column: When confidence is medium or low, this column explains exactly what the AI was uncertain about.

Known Gaps and Limitations

The following are known limitations of the tool that abstractors should be aware of before exporting.

Duplicate Rows from Multi-Page PDFs

When a single PDF spans multiple pages and contains repeated heading or identification information, the AI may extract the same instrument twice. This results in two rows with the same VOL/PAGE in the review table.

Always scan for duplicate VOL/PAGE values in the review table before exporting. Delete the duplicate row using the Delete button. This is most common with deed book scans that include a cover page stamp on every page.

Judgments and Liens Not Extracted

The app only processes deed book PDF scans. It does not extract judgments, tax liens, UCC filings, or other lien-related instruments because these come from separate court and UCC record searches, not deed book pages.

Judgment and lien rows must be added manually in the review table or directly in the exported Excel file after export. Reference the separate judgments search document provided by the abstractor.

Legal Descriptions

Complex metes and bounds descriptions, especially those involving multiple parcels or exceptions, are frequently incomplete or inaccurate. Always verify legal descriptions against the source document before exporting.

Handwritten Documents

OCR accuracy on cursive handwriting is limited. Deeds from before approximately 1950 that are handwritten should be treated as starting points only and require careful manual review.

County Book Prefix Conventions

Different counties use different deed book prefixes. Greene County PA uses "RB" (Record Book) while Allegheny County uses "DB" (Deed Book). The AI reads whatever prefix appears in the source document. If your county convention requires a specific prefix throughout, update the VOL/PAGE field in the review table before exporting.

Corrective Deed Identification

The AI correctly identifies the instrument type (e.g. General Warranty Deed) but may not always append "Corrective" when a deed is a corrective instrument. If a deed corrects a prior instrument, add "Corrective" to the instrument type in the review table: General Warranty Corrective Deed.

Tips and Best Practices

For best OCR results

Higher DPI scans produce better results. Scan at 300 DPI or above when possible.
Straighten pages before scanning. Rotated or skewed pages reduce OCR accuracy, especially on handwritten documents.

What the AI does well

Typed instruments from the 1950s onward extract reliably with high confidence in most cases.
Standard deed language — grantor, grantee, volume/page, and recorded date — is almost always captured correctly on clear scans.
Complex corporate conveyances including bankruptcy deeds, corrective deeds, and name change certificates are handled well.

What the AI struggles with

Legal descriptions are frequently incomplete or inaccurate. Always verify against the source document.
Handwritten documents require significant manual correction. OCR on cursive handwriting has inherent limitations.
Multi-page PDFs may produce duplicate rows. Always check the review table for duplicate VOL/PAGE values before exporting.

Frequently Asked Questions

I see the same VOL/PAGE appear twice — is that a bug?
No — this is a known behavior with multi-page PDFs. When a scanned deed book page includes a repeated header stamp or identification line on each page, the AI may read it as two separate instruments. Simply click Delete on the duplicate row before exporting. You can spot duplicates quickly by scanning the VOL/PAGE column for repeated values.
The final product has a Judgments row but the app doesn't extract one — why?
The app only processes deed book PDF scans. Judgments, tax liens, UCC filings, and other lien instruments come from separate court and UCC record searches and are not contained in deed book pages. These rows must be added manually in the review table or directly in the exported Excel file. Reference the separate judgments search document.
The VOL/PAGE prefix is wrong for my county — how do I fix it?
Different counties use different deed book prefixes (DB, RB, OB, OR, etc.). The AI reads whatever prefix appears in the source document. If your county convention requires a specific prefix throughout — for example Greene County PA uses "RB" — simply click into the VOL/PAGE field in the review table and update the prefix before exporting. The volume and page numbers themselves will always be correct.
I uploaded 10 files but got 17 rows — is that normal?
Yes. A single PDF can contain multiple recorded instruments. The AI extracts each primary instrument as a separate row. Review the Source column to see which file each row came from, and delete any rows that are not part of your chain of title. Prior references cited within an instrument should not appear as separate rows — if they do, delete them.
The instrument type says "Deed" instead of the full type — how do I fix it?
The AI is instructed to always specify the full deed type (General Warranty Deed, Special Warranty Deed, etc.). If it returns just "Deed", the deed type was likely not stated in the document or the scan was unclear. Check the source document and correct manually in the review table. Also, if the deed is a corrective instrument, add "Corrective" to the type: General Warranty Corrective Deed.
What is the difference between Doc Date and Recorded Date sorting?
Doc Date is when the instrument was signed or executed. Recorded Date is when it was filed with the county recorder. For chain of title purposes, Recorded Date is typically preferred. Both dates always appear in the export regardless of which sort field you choose.
How long does processing take?
Typically 10 to 30 seconds per file. Since files are processed in parallel, a batch of 25 files takes about the same time as a batch of 5. Very long or heavily degraded PDFs may take longer.
Is my data stored anywhere?
PDFs are temporarily stored in Vercel Blob storage during processing only. They are not retained long-term. Extracted data exists only in your browser session — nothing is saved to a database. Closing or refreshing the page will clear all extracted data.
